01.Overview

Marmix is a ready-mix concrete manufacturer near Lublin, delivering concrete and building materials within roughly a 40 km radius to two very different audiences: construction firms working to a schedule, and individual investors pouring their first foundation.

Their website dated back to 2015 and no longer matched the scale or professionalism of the business, so Marmix asked for a site that would win more work — not just look nicer. I took the project end to end: auditing the old site, running the research, restructuring the information architecture, designing the UI in Figma, and building the new site in WordPress and Elementor myself.

02.Problem

The old site quietly worked against the business. A dark, cramped 2015 theme made a serious manufacturer look like a hobby project; eight competing menu items buried the offer under walls of SEO text and stacked tabs; the gallery showed broken image placeholders; and the phone number sat passively in a corner with no clear call to action.

The genuinely useful concrete calculator was hidden inside a bare, unlabelled form, and the contact page led with GDPR copy instead of an easy way to get in touch. Nothing answered the questions buyers actually ask — delivery area, concrete classes, pump availability — so neither the contractor on a live site nor the first-time investor was served.

The 2015 site across its key pages — offer, gallery, calculator and contact.

03.Design Process

I began with a full heuristic audit and a competitive scan of regional suppliers, then reframed the whole project around the two buyers — the contractor who decides fast, and the individual investor who needs guidance and reassurance.

From there I restructured the information architecture, collapsing eight scattered menu items into a focused six and grouping the entire range under one clear “Oferta” section with dedicated product subpages. I moved from wireframes to high-fidelity UI in Figma, designing mobile-first, and built the approved screens in WordPress and Elementor.

A few decisions did most of the work: a single message and one “Zadzwoń teraz” action in the hero; the offer as a scannable six-tile product grid; a simple three-step ordering path (get in touch → choose the product → receive the delivery); and trust made visible through benefits, certificates, EU-funding marks and an FAQ.

Pictured above: views of the final approved designs. (Left to right: homepage, offer, product page, concrete calculator, contact.)

04.Solution & Results

The result is a site that's modern, trustworthy and built to convert. The offer is finally legible, every page leads toward a call or an enquiry, and the calculator gives buyers a reason to engage before they even get in touch. Because it's built in WordPress, the team keeps it up to date themselves.
